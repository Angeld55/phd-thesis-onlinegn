	\textbf{The dissertation thesis contains:} \\
	182 pages, 85 figures, 25 code fragments, and 19 formulas.
	
	\vspace{0.3cm}
	
	\textbf{The bibliography includes:} 104 references.
	
	\vspace{0.3cm}
	
	\textbf{The structure of the dissertation includes:} Introduction, 4 chapters, Conclusion, Author's statement of contributions, List of publications related to the dissertation topic, and Bibliography.
	
	\begin{itemize}
		\item The \textbf{Introduction} substantiates the relevance of the topic and defines the object and the subject of the research.
		\item \textbf{Chapter 1} presents the theoretical foundations of Generalized Nets (GNs), analyzes the existing software for simulation, and formulates the aim and objectives of the dissertation thesis.
		\item \textbf{Chapter 2} is devoted to newly developed algorithms for automatic drawing of GNs and transformation of graphical images into \textit{TeX} format.
		\item \textbf{Chapter 3} describes the architecture, design, and implementation of the developed software simulator \textit{OnlineGN}.
		\item \textbf{Chapter 4} examines the creation of OM models for processes to which OM have not been applied so far or for which other approaches have been used, as well as the applications of the simulator, with each of the specified models being simulated and verified through it.
		\item \textbf{The Conclusion} summarizes the achieved results and outlines directions for future work.
	\end{itemize}
